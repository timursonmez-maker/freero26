The Wheel of Fortune slot — CCL, Marylou, Nono Gigsta & rRoxymore
All four appear in this guide. Based on CCL's description of their last FR set ("imagining a psychic electric swamp and how one would levitate through it") and the fact that Nono Gigsta's FR2024 set began and ended with Björk and is the most lauded mix of 2024, this is the centrepiece of the weekend for you. Plan everything else around it.
Essential — start here
Nono Gigsta
Freerotation 2024: The House of Crocodiles Pt.2
The single most celebrated mix of 2024, named independently by RA, DJ Mag, Mixmag and Passion of the Weiss. Starts with Björk's "I Miss You", ends with Led Zeppelin's "Whole Lotta Love", and in between folds nature documentaries, R&B, dubbed horns and soundsystem bass into something that defies accounting for. Nono Gigsta holds a PhD on the "unspeakability" of electronic music — the set sounds like that research in practice. She returns to FR26 in the Wheel of Fortune slot.
↗ House of Crocodiles Pt.2 — FR2024 ↗ House of Crocodiles Pt.1 — FR2018 ↗ Full archive
CCL
Freerotation 2024 (Animalia) + "A Night in the Skull Discotheque"
CCL on their FR2024 set: "sexy, wonky, trippy — imagining a psychic electric swamp and how one would levitate through it." The Skull Discotheque mixtape (T4T LUV NRG) is a 90-minute excavation of proto-dubstep from the late 70s to 2000, described as "a majestic haunted dancehall." Self-described practice: "liquefaction of rhythmic and melodic matter — trip-no, dub-core, cowgirl-breaks, drum & face." One of the most serious selectors working. In the Wheel of Fortune slot.

Steevio & Suzybee
Steevio live modular sets on Bandcamp + SoundCloud
Steevio built Freerotation. He performs live on a modular rig he calls Keith Emerson-esque in scale. His own words: "I got bored of the same 4/4 motifs. I wanted deeper, more hypnotic, tripped-out stuff." Works microtonally and in just intonation — slippery melodies, subtly broken beats, afro-latin undertones. One-take live improvisations. The Modular Techno series on Mindtours is the production entry point; his SoundCloud has the live recordings.

Jane Fitz
RA Live @ Houghton 2025 + Bonanza closing 2024 + Japan Rural 2024
A DJ's DJ. Sets are "a deep and winding journey between techno, acid, abstract experimental and ambient — often stretching for multiple hours." Committed vinyl digger. Her Japan Rural b2b (2024) tracklist includes Konduku, Rhythm & Sound, Wave Arising and dub techno deep house — a precise overlap with your territory. The RA Live Houghton 2025 (sunrise, 3 hours) is the best recent entry point.

Azu Tiwaline b2b Forest Drive West
Crack Mix 397 + "Fluids in Motion" EP (Livity Sound) + Lentekabinet 2024
Tunisian-French, living between the Sahara and France. Draws on Berber and transe music, dub and polyrhythms. Ideal listening: barefoot outdoors, quadraphonic subs, head in the sky. The Livity Sound EP with Forest Drive West ranges "from pure ambience through to spring-loaded 4/4, with a focus on minimalism and dub-spirited spatial sound design." Their b2b at FR26 is a different proposition to either solo — the overlap zone is where you want to be.

Konduku
RA Podcast 967 — Dec 2024 + Houghton 2025
RA: "distinctly angled rhythms, favouring subtle textures over hooks and riffs." Known for moving De School to tears. RA.967 is 90 minutes of "long layered blends, locked grooves and spine-tingling atmospherics." His 2019 Gegek EP is UK beat-science territory à la Peverelist; his 2023 Hayal EP goes deep Nobu-core techno. Neither is hard. Oblique, psychedelic, groove-first — the rhythm is the emotion.

Joe Ellis
Freerotation 2024 + FR2018 (UntilMyHeartStops label)
Freerotation resident — one of the core Welsh crew that built the festival with Steevio. Steevio's exact words: "Why is this guy not a famous DJ? He just seems to see through the music and see what a lot of other people can't see." Behind UntilMyHeartStops and Lamassu labels. Very little internet footprint; the FR recordings on the label SoundCloud are the direct way in.

Marylou

Cousin
Sustain Release 2024 closing + naffcast003 (Priori b2b) + Boiler Room Sydney
Sydney-based, Moonshoe label. Broken, textural and psychedelic — not hard, not straight. DJ Mag on his Sustain Release 2024 set: "dub techno echoes swirl over Latin club rollers, deep house thumpers and cosmic bruk, all maintaining appropriate restraint while gesturing toward the psychedelic potential of peak time." The naffcast Priori b2b — recorded 6am–1pm on what was billed as "an ambient stage" but wasn't — is the best discovery-mode listen. Boiler Room Sydney gives you him in a harder room than FR, useful for range.

Steevio
North Wales, UK
your zone
Co-founder of Freerotation with his partner Suzybee, who creates the festival's hand-drawn animated visuals. He has been involved in electronic music since managing the UK's first electro/breakdance club in Newcastle in 1982. Performs live on a Keith Emerson-scale modular rig built since 2009. Everything is improvised in one take, no computer. His music works microtonally, in just intonation, with slippery melodies and subtly broken beats.
“The plan is to make Freerotation a worker's co-operative eventually, and we are well on the way to that goal.”— Oval Space interview
