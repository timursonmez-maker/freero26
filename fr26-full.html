▸ Your anchor — start with these
Sybil self-describes her mixes as "a dive into the ocean — flowing between psychedelia of the 70s, 90s, present-day and beyond." Cinematic techno + deep ambient journeys. Genre-fluid: downtempo → breaks → trance → D&B without ever hardening.

Must listen — confirmed close to Sybil's world
Nono Gigsta
Freerotation 2024 : The House of Crocodiles Pt.2 (live recording)
This was the most celebrated mix of 2024 across RA, DJ Mag, Mixmag and Passion of the Weiss — all unprompted. Starts with Björk, ends with Led Zeppelin's "Whole Lotta Love", and in between collapses nature documentaries, dubbed horn sections, R&B and soundsystem-melting bass drops into a single dream logic. Nono Gigsta has a PhD on the "unspeakability" of electronic music; the set sounds like that research in practice. She's also returning to Freerotation 2026 as part of the Wheel of Fortune. This is the single most important listen on this list.
↗ House of Crocodiles Pt.2 — Freerotation 2024 ↗ House of Crocodiles Pt.1 — Freerotation 2018 ↗ Full SoundCloud archive
CCL
Freerotation 2024 (Animalia series) + "A Night in the Skull Discotheque"
CCL's own words on their Freerotation 2024 set: "I wanted to keep it sexy, wonky, trippy — imagining a psychic electric swamp and how one would levitate through it." They describe their practice as "liquefaction of rhythmic and melodic matter — combustive phase transitions through wiggle-steppers, trip-no, dub-core, cowgirl-breaks." The Skull Discotheque mixtape (T4T LUV NRG label) is a 90-minute excavation of proto-dubstep from the 70s to 2000, described as "a majestic haunted dancehall." They're back on FR26 as part of Wheel of Fortune with rRoxymore, Marylou, Nono Gigsta. The whole Wheel of Fortune slot is going to be something.
↗ CCL @ Freerotation 2024 (Animalia) ↗ CCL SoundCloud — find Skull Discotheque + liquidtime radio
Azu Tiwaline b2b Forest Drive West
Crack Mix 397 + "Fluids in Motion" EP on Livity Sound + Lentekabinet 2024
Tunisian-French artist living between the Sahara and France. She draws on Berber and transe music, dub, techno and what she calls "primitive polyrhythmic beats." Her ideal listening position: barefoot, outdoors, head in the sky, quadraphonic sound system with many subs. The Crack Mix was built "dreaming of a future dancefloor, dancing under the stars." Her collaborative EP with Forest Drive West (Livity Sound, 2024) explores "a variety of tempos and rhythms ranging from pure ambience through to spring-loaded 4/4, with a focus on minimalism and dub-spirited spatial sound design." She's booked for FR26 in b2b with Forest Drive West, which is a very different proposition to either of them solo — expect the overlap zone.

Konduku
RA Podcast 967 (Dec 2024) + Houghton 2025
RA description: "He deals in detailed, distinctly angled rhythms, favouring subtle textures over hooks and riffs." Known for once moving De School to tears. His RA podcast is 90 minutes of "long layered blends, flick-of-the-wrist transitions, locked grooves and spine-tingling atmospherics." His Gegek EP (2019) sits in UK beat science territory à la Peverelist; his Hayal EP (2023) goes Nobu-core deep techno. Neither is hard — both are hypnotic. The rhythm is the emotion. Sybil and Konduku occupy the same axis: oblique, psychedelic, not about drops.
↗ RA Podcast 967 — Dec 2024 ↗ Houghton 2025 ↗ Konduku mixes archive
Marylou
Crack Mix 599 (2025) + Morphine Raum sets
French, Berlin-based, Morphine Raum affiliate. Crack's description: "From improv jazz and noise to footwork, breakcore, bass and dub, her sets jump through genres and eras as much as BPMs, crafting playful, psychedelic journeys that take crowds to unexpected places." Her own framing: "The club needs no codes, total freedom. My perception of a DJ set is definitely not common." Also on the FR26 lineup as part of Wheel of Fortune (b2b Azu Tiwaline). Very few people playing footwork, jazz and dub in a single set and making it feel earned rather than chaotic.
↗ Search "Crack Mix 599 Marylou" on SoundCloud ↗ Also: Wheel of Fortune collaborator Nono Gigsta @ FR24
Very likely — investigated and earned
Polygonia
Waking Life 2025 + Lot Radio 2024 + Fusion 2024 live
Munich-based Lindsey Wang. Plays violin and Chinese Xiao flute, weaves them into electronic production. RA: "earthy psychedelia brushes up against precision-engineered rhythm." New EP on Timedance (March 2026) is described as "restless percussion and subtly warped harmonies, threaded by a quietly hallucinatory sense of detail." Her Lot Radio 2024 set is described as "organic mix of grey area grooves, mind-bending sound design." The Festival sets (Fusion, Waking Life) give you her full arc; she builds worlds rather than peaks.
↗ Waking Life 2025 ↗ Lot Radio 2024 — "grey area grooves" ↗ Fusion 2024 live set ↗ Monument 2024 — Kia b2b Polygonia
Garçon
Waking Life 2022 + Organik Festival 2018 + Parallel Festival 2023
Basel-based. Runs Amenthia Recordings (label releasing Konduku, Shackleton, basic chanel — a good indicator). RA called his 2023 set "pure, hypnotic class." Description: "He teases music gradually and deliberately, layering hypnotic grooves atop one another and subtly modulating the mood of the night — with this delayed gratification leading to a far greater payoff." His Organik 2018 set tracklist includes Brian Eno & Jon Hassell, Rainforest Spiritual Enslavement, Coil, Monolake — that's a precise map of where his head is. One of the most genuinely under-known names on this lineup.
↗ Garçon @ Waking Life 2022 ↗ Organik Festival 2018 (Eno/Hassell, Coil, Monolake) ↗ Parallel Festival 2023 — b2b Agonis ↗ Full SoundCloud archive
Tripcode
Petrichor LP (Bandcamp) — Fernando Jarabo, FR resident
Freerotation festival's own resident. Modular synthesist and "eclectic vinyl selector." His Petrichor LP is three tracks of slow, textured, unclassifiable electronic music — the kind of thing that works in a field at 4am or a listening room on a Tuesday. Very little internet footprint, which is usually the right sign. Go to the Bandcamp then find his SoundCloud for the selector side.
↗ Petrichor LP — Bandcamp ↗ Tripcode on SoundCloud
Djrum
NTS guest mix May 2025 (album release mix)
The NTS mix — made for the release of "Under Tangled Silence" — is the version of Djrum closest to Sybil's world. Quieter, more introspective, less about the three-decks-all-vinyl feat and more about what the records mean. The album itself is worth the full listen; it's been building for six years.

Discovery — artists you probably don't know yet
These are artists I found by following connections outward from the FR26 lineup and the world Sybil moves in. Less covered, worth going in cold.
rRoxymore
Freerotation 2017 DJ set + SoundCloud archive
On FR26 as part of the Wheel of Fortune. rRoxymore's sets dissolve rather than drop — things come apart at the seams in a way that feels intentional. The FR 2017 recording is the best existing reference point for how they work in this specific environment. Their style sits in a Björk-adjacent emotional-electronic space: structured but weird, warm but off-axis. A natural companion to Sybil.
↗ rRoxymore @ Freerotation 2017 ↗ Full archive
Kin Teal
"Hasonmás" (Tropopause) + "Piqued" (Mixmag) + "Undercurrent"
Production-side, few DJ recordings available. Textural, groove-inflected, IDM-adjacent with emotional precision. "Hasonmás" is the entry point — slow-moving but never inert, sounds like memory. On the shimmerier edge of your profile but with enough rhythmic architecture to stay interesting. Tropopause label is worth following as a whole.
↗ "Hasonmás" — Tropopause premiere ↗ "Piqued" — Mixmag premiere ↗ "Undercurrent"
Pariah
@ Freerotation 2024 — same venue, last year
Heard his FR2024 set at the same Baskerville Hall. Pariah's emotional register is closer to Sybil than most Hessle-adjacent artists — he's more interested in feeling than cleverness. Go in at the 40-minute mark. The Freerotation recording is useful precisely because you can hear how he reads this particular room and crowd.
↗ Pariah @ Freerotation 2024
The Wheel of Fortune slot
FR26 lists Wheel of Fortune as: CCL, Marylou, Nono Gigsta & rRoxymore — all four of whom appear in this guide. That's a combined slot, and based on precedent (CCL have described previous Wheel of Fortune sets as "psychic electric swamp" events), this will be the centrepiece of the weekend for you. The 2024 Nono Gigsta set began and ended with Björk and used this slot to reconstruct the room's collective memory. Worth planning your whole weekend schedule around.
Probably not for you, on this lineup:

Surgeon — has played beautiful ambient sets at Freerotation before, but default mode is industrial/driving. Unpredictable.
Move D — 4/4 house risk is real. His FR2024 set is on SoundCloud if you want to verify first.
Pangaea / Pearson Sound / Ben UFO — Hessle Audio groove is physical and clever, not emotional in the Sybil sense. Cold by comparison.
Peverelist — Bristol warmth but monotone. More body than feeling.
